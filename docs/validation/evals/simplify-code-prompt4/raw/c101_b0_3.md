# C1-01 Ordered Selection — B0 Control Sample 3

## Shared boundary

- Mode: one bounded read-only Simplify Code decision.
- Preserved contract: the caller-visible behavior represented by the supplied
  caller-facing fixture.
- Proof boundary: establish the fixture against the exact starting state and
  require the same fixture result after the proposed cut, followed by the
  nearest relevant checks. The packet supplies no executed proof result, so
  each selection is conditional on that before/after proof.
- State boundary: no files, index entries, dependencies, installed state, or
  external state are changed here. Any later implementation is one bounded
  unstaged cut, limited to its directly created fallout, with unrelated work
  and the index preserved as found.

## Positive case

**Selected cut:** Delete the expired branch proven unreachable.

**Why this cut:** Every candidate is admissible, and deletion is the first
admissible rung. The reachability trace supplies the semantic basis for removal;
the caller-facing fixture supplies the observable behavior seam.

**Stop:** Stop after this single deletion and its directly created fallout.
Do not proceed to Reuse, Standardize, Collapse, or Shrink because the mode owns
one coherent cut.

## Companion 1 — Delete excluded by authoritative semantics

**Selected cut:** Reuse the exact project-owned semantic owner.

**Why this cut:** The authoritative semantic commitment prevents deleting the
branch. Exact reuse is therefore the first admissible rung, provided the owner
matches the required semantics and the fixture remains unchanged.

**Stop:** Stop after replacing the duplicate responsibility with that owner and
removing only displaced private fallout. Do not continue to later rungs.

## Companion 2 — Reuse excluded by compatibility

**Selected cut:** Use the runtime-native exact match.

**Why this cut:** Deletion is already unavailable, and the project-owned owner
cannot be reused without violating compatibility. The runtime-native operation
is the first remaining admissible rung only if its contract is exact at the
supported compatibility floor.

**Stop:** Stop after the native substitution and directly displaced fallout.
Do not collapse or shrink additional code.

## Companion 3 — Standardize excluded by edge behavior

**Selected cut:** Collapse the private abstraction that earns no boundary.

**Why this cut:** The earlier rungs are unavailable, and the runtime-native
operation does not preserve required edge behavior. Collapsing the private
abstraction is the first remaining admissible cut when its callers and behavior
fit wholly inside the bounded target.

**Stop:** Stop after inlining or removing that private boundary and its direct
fallout. Preserve the edge-behavior fixture and do not also shrink the loop.

## Companion 4 — Collapse excluded by coherence

**Selected cut:** Shrink the loop while preserving behavior.

**Why this cut:** Deletion, reuse, and native standardization are unavailable,
and removing the abstraction would break a coherent responsibility boundary.
The loop reduction is the first remaining admissible rung.

**Stop:** Stop after the loop-only reduction. Preserve ordering, timing, and
edge behavior through the caller-facing fixture; retain the coherent
abstraction.

## Companion 5 — Proof condition excludes the remaining cut

**Selected outcome:** **No safe simplification**.

**Why this outcome:** The earlier rungs are unavailable and the supplied proof
condition cannot establish that the remaining loop reduction preserves the
caller-visible contract. An unproved cut cannot authorize mutation.

**Stop:** Stop before production mutation. The residual belongs with the owner
of the missing or inadequate caller-facing proof; the starting work state
remains unchanged.
