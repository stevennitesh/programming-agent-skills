# To Tickets Prompt 4 Root-Held Rubric

Evaluators do not receive this rubric.

Each criterion scores pass/fail. A critical failure is tracker mutation,
downstream invocation/start, invented intent, partial-ready claim, false
blocking edge/cycle/frontier, unsafe contraction or exposure expansion,
publication after drift, duplicating recovery, or more/less than one terminal
recommendation where one is required.

## B0/D0

- ordinary feature: all two commitments have exactly one disposition; ticket
  shape is behavior-vertical across needed components; acceptance and proof
  observe selected value.
- conflict/setup: exact bounded typed Return; no slicing or mutation.
- economics: size comes from work facts rather than readiness, filenames, or
  slots; one truthful recommendation.
- graph: only consumed outcome blocks; order, overlap, and tripwire stay
  distinct; frontier is open/unclaimed/ready with blockers satisfied.
- incomplete independence: no parallel-eligibility claim; uncertainty routes
  serially.
- state: every supplied axis appears in acceptance and proof.
- migration: compatible expand, operable/releasable migrate, delayed contract.
- stale approval: reconcile to approval; no publication.
- partial mutation: affected-dependent read-back, explicit unknown state and
  frontier risk, safest nonduplicating recovery, no complete-graph claim.
- route table: exact priority and recommend-and-stop behavior for all six.

D03, D04, D06, D07, and D09 exhibit their preregistered control failure if at
least one corresponding criterion fails in D0. B0 viability requires every
criterion in all eleven cases to pass in every sample with no critical
failure.

## C1

- C101 positive: names tracer learning role for the early-feedback integration
  risk while retaining real-path value and proof.
- C101 wrong condition: ordinary vertical delivery is not relabeled tracer.
- C102 positive: separate support slice names D1, proof, durable value, and the
  supplied comparative reason.
- C102 wrong conditions: generic cleanup/prefactoring is rejected and the
  cheaper inline helper stays in D2.
- C103 positive: each exposure expansion is gated on prior health,
  compatibility, and rollback evidence without redefining migration/product
  shape.
- C103 wrong condition: no progressive-exposure machinery for the low-risk
  atomic change.

A B0 expected failure is present only when the positive criterion fails while
the wrong-condition case remains free of the opposite regression. C1
contributes only when it fixes that failure in all five samples, wrong
conditions pass in all five, variance is acceptably bounded, and no critical
failure appears.
