The supplied facts say caller admission is absent, and the blocking batch is mixed because F2 is still `decision-required`; the runtime contract gives a mixed or decision-required blocking set no Repair authority even though F1 is individually automatic.

## First moment Repair may open

Repair may first open only after the intact report has been returned and the caller newly admits the complete blocking set exactly as `{F1, F2}`. At that moment all supplied admission gates are satisfied:

- **Completeness/equality:** the supplied blocking set is exactly `{F1, F2}`, and the caller admits exactly `{F1, F2}`; F3 is expressly advisory and nonblocking.
- **Automatic scope:** the supplied post-admission facts classify both F1 and F2 as `automatic-in-scope`.
- **F1 Charter gate:** the supplied remedy evidence says the admitted F1 remedy remains within the frozen Charter and changes no commitment.
- **F2 Charter gate:** the supplied caller-authorized disposition says the F2 correction remains within the frozen Charter and changes no commitment.
- **Repair budget:** the supplied facts leave one Repair generation.
- **Successor-review budget:** the supplied facts leave one successor-review invocation.
- **Finding/proof packet:** the supplied formal report is complete, and F1 expressly carries concrete defect evidence and required proof; the Repair assignment must carry the complete admitted finding set and required proof and may change only F1 and F2 under the original frozen Charter.

Thus the exact Repair generation is one bounded batch for the admitted blocker IDs **F1 and F2**. F3 authorizes no work.

The supplied remaining successor-review invocation is consumed by that fresh H2 review; H1's report cannot approve H2.
