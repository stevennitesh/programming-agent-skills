# A-03 — Family A Simulation

## Supplied-fact legend

- **[Authority]** “Simulate only from the supplied facts” and perform no real tracker, Git, worktree, remote, installation, publication, or destructive mutation.
- **[Boundary]** Read the exact runtime and fixture; write only this disposable capture.
- **[A-request]** The request is an explicit top-level delivery of parent `P-41` through its complete, non-empty, exhaustive Ready-for-agent graph.
- **[A-setup]** All required repository setup surfaces exist and are compatible.
- **[A-graph]** Children `A`, `B`, `C`, and `D` are Ready, have complete execution profiles and state matrices, and carry acceptance and proof requirements. `D` depends on `C`.
- **[A-isolation]** `A` and `B` share a semantic invariant, overlapping production writes, and `exclusive-db`; `C` is independently bounded; `D` is bounded but cannot land before `C`.
- **[A-open]** `A`’s claim reads back as owned by another actor; `B`’s claim reads back exactly but its Python project origin is outside its worktree; `C` and `D` have exact claims and ready isolated-lane packets.
- **[A-returns]** Valid `C` and `D` workers return out of dependency order. `C` returns a bounded, clean, proved commit from the frozen base. `D` returns a bounded, proved commit whose base predates the accepted `C` landing.
- **[A-conflict]** Attempting to land `C` enters a merge conflict and preserves partial Git-operation state.
- **[Runtime]** The supplied runtime requires root-only gate ownership, canonical ledger evidence, pairwise concurrency qualification, exact claim/preflight read-back, serial landing, current-HEAD proof, preserved conflict routing, formal review, child-first Lock, and safe lane/claim accounting.

## Allowed simulated mutations and retained state

The only admissible simulated state changes are: scope/frontier records; exact claim observations; lane-create/ready records for `C/D`; a lane-created-but-preflight-blocked record for `B`; dispatch, handoff, and acceptance for `C/D`; the preserved conflict record for `C`; and, only after its required evidence exists, a resumable checkpoint. This set follows the fixture’s allowed operations plus **[Authority]**, **[A-open]**, **[A-returns]**, **[A-conflict]**, and **[Runtime]**.

Retained state at the stop:

- `A`: open and campaign-unclaimed; blocked by another actor’s claim (**[A-open]**).
- `B`: open and not dispatched; exact campaign claim observed, created lane preserved at provenance-preflight failure; its claim must be either released with read-back or retained with owner/token/time/recovery-owner evidence before checkpoint (**[A-open]**, **[Runtime]**).
- `C`: accepted worker commit preserved, but not landed because the landing operation is conflicted (**[A-returns]**, **[A-conflict]**).
- `D`: accepted worker commit preserved and not landed; waits on successful `C` landing and stale-base inspection (**[A-isolation]**, **[A-returns]**).
- Integration: partial conflict state preserved; no invented integration SHA, tracker closeout, review decision, publication evidence, or completion claim (**[A-conflict]**, **[Authority]**, **[Runtime]**).

## Overlay transitions

1. Initial execution states are pending/ready; no tracker item is closed (**[A-graph]**, **[Runtime]**).
2. After return acceptance, `C` and `D` are accepted/committed-unlanded. Acceptance alone creates no dependency overlay (**[A-returns]**, **[Runtime]**).
3. The conflicted `C` landing creates **no** `landed-awaiting-lock` overlay because no clean, proved integration landing exists (**[A-conflict]**, **[Runtime]**).
4. If conflict recovery later produces a clean proved successor HEAD `Hc`, the root may record a campaign-scoped overlay keyed by `(C, Hc, proof-C)`. That overlay makes the `C -> D` landing dependency ready but does not close `C` in the tracker (**[A-graph]**, **[A-isolation]**, **[Runtime]**).
5. Even with that overlay, `D` cannot land until its pre-`Hc` base is inspected for stale overlap/conflict and the recombined proof passes (**[A-returns]**, **[Runtime]**).
6. Rollback of `Hc`, invalidation, or failed proof removes the `C` overlay and reblocks `D`; tracker dependency state remains unchanged until Lock (**[Runtime]**).

## Final disposition

**`blocked`, resumable after evidence repair; not terminal-complete.** The immediate recovery boundary is the preserved `C` merge conflict. Resume by reconciling the conflict operation, actors, claims, lanes, tracker, remote, and current integration HEAD; route the conflict through `$resolving-merge-conflicts`; then re-run focused and recombined proof before recording any `C` landing or overlay. `D` follows only after stale-base inspection. `A` and `B` remain open until their exact claim/provenance blockers change. This disposition is required by **[A-open]**, **[A-returns]**, **[A-conflict]**, and the nonterminal preservation rules in **[Runtime]**.
