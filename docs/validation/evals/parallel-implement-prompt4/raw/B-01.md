## Ordered gate decisions

### 0. Mutation boundary — allow simulation, deny every real mutation

**Decision:** The only concrete write is this capture. All later “record,”
“release,” “repair,” “close,” “clean,” and “review” actions are hypothetical
state transitions, not operations against the repository or external systems.
This gate is allowed because the requested task is a simulation and the
capture is the sole authorized mutation. [C-A, C-M, C-O]

**Allowed simulated mutations:** hypothetical canonical-event receipts,
overlay changes, proof/review records, Repair records, claim/lane dispositions,
and tracker closeout/read-back records. [C-A, C-O]

### 1. Admission — admit resume at the root

**Decision:** Admit P-77 as a root-owned resume, not as a delegated worker
campaign. The fact says both “explicitly requested” and “root campaign,” which
satisfies the runtime's root-only admission boundary. No supplied fact
authorizes changing the parent scope or reconstructing its graph. [B-R, R-T]

**Progression evidence required:** canonical P-77 scope/Charter and exhaustive
graph must already be present in the canonical events; if reconciliation
reveals that they are absent or contradictory, route the complete graph defect
back to the ticket-shaping owner rather than infer missing scope. [B-S, R-T]

closed” from the generated projection because it conflicts with the
authoritative canonical events and tracker read-back. The correct execution
projection is `landed-awaiting-lock`, not tracker-closed. [B-S, B-A, B-P, R-T,
R-S]

**Decision:** Do not dispatch, land, review, repair, Lock, close trackers, or
publish yet. Resume authority remains closed because actor, claim, worktree,

B-X4, B-H, R-T]

**Exact blockers and routes:**

### 3. Select/overlay reconciliation — restore or remove A's overlay by proof

**Decision:** During unresolved reconciliation, do not consume A's overlay to
unlock dependents even though the canonical event retains the recorded
landing. After complete reconciliation, use `landed-awaiting-lock` only if A's
landing is still in current integration history and the identity-matched
current proof covers it. [B-A, B-PR, R-S]

**Overlay transitions:**

1. Canonical start: `A = landed-awaiting-lock`; tracker remains open. [B-A]
2. Conflicting projection: no transition to `closed`; the projection is
   rejected. [B-P, R-T]
3. Resume uncertainty: overlay is retained as recorded evidence but is not
   usable for new readiness until current ancestry and proof reconcile. [B-H,
   R-T, R-S]
4. Reconciled success: if A remains in current integration history, restore
   usable `landed-awaiting-lock` on the identity-matched proof. [B-PR, R-S]
5. Reconciled rollback, invalidation, missing ancestry, or failed proof: remove
   the overlay and reblock every dependent; do not mark A done. [R-S]
6. Only verified child closeout with tracker mutation read-back may transition
   A from the overlay to `closed`. [B-A, R-L]

### 8. Lock/closeout attempt — reject parent-first closure and preserve B's partial state

**Decision:** The later closeout attempt is not authorized by the supplied
facts because a passing successor review is not supplied. Independently, the
parent-first attempt violates child-first Lock ordering. Do not accept a
parent-closeout receipt. [B-SR, B-CO, R-L]

**Decision:** Treat B's partial update as real observed external state for the
simulation, not as success and not as permission to undo it. Preserve and
account for the exact intended mutation, posted/observed result, and tracker
read-back. B is not `verified` until every required closeout field and
read-back agree. [B-CO, R-L, R-C]

**Overlay transitions at closeout:** A remains `landed-awaiting-lock` and
tracker-open unless a later authorized child-A closeout is verified with
read-back. B becomes `partial-closeout-observed`, not `closed`. The parent
remains open/unverified; an attempted parent-first mutation must be read back
and, if it changed external state, escalated to the tracker owner for
reconciliation rather than silently treated as valid or rolled back. [B-A,
B-P, B-CO, C-A, R-L, R-C]

**Exact recovery route:**

1. Reconcile the actual child-B and parent tracker states and preserve the

2. Resume at the earliest unmet Review gate: resolve F2; if eligible, complete

4. Close and read back every child, including A and B, before any parent

   preserved, and verify separately authorized applicable publication

never resume from parent closeout. [B-S, B-A, B-BU, B-RV, B-CO, R-T, R-C]
